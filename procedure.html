Procedure


1

Setting Up The Plants In The Containers

  • Each of the three species of plants in the six experimental groups were precisely weighed and then divided evenly into six parts (14g each)
  • The plants were split into one plant containers and two plant combination containers

2

Making Solutions

  • Six liters of ammonia solution was made by taking a 28 - 30% ammonium hydroxide solution and diluting it.
  • Twelve liters of lead solution was made in a volumetric flask by diluting 0.0216 grams of lead with 1.2 liters of distilled water, making the concentration 0.0018 grams lead per 100 mL.

3

Setting Up The Plants in Contaminated Water Containers

  • Each container was filled with 2 liters of ammonia solution and 200 mL of lead solution.
  • The plants were moved to the contaminated water containers using beakers
  • The ammonia, pH, nitrate, lead, sodium chloride, and iron levels of the water mixture were tested and recorded

4

Experiment Procedures and Data Collection

  • Every two to three days, the ammonia, pH, nitrate, lead, sodium chloride, and iron levels of the water in each container were recorded.
  • The pH was measured with a pH sensor connected to ​​LabQuest 2 software
  • The ammonia lead, sodium chloride, and iron levels were measured with test tubes and strips, and the colors of the test strip were compared to the ones on the bottle
